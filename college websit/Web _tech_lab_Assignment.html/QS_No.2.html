1. Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the south bank
of the Yamuna river in Agra.

2. India Gate

The India Gate in New Delhi was built between 1921 and 1931.Designed
by Sir Edwin Lutyens, the foundation stone was laid on February 10, 1921,
and it was inaugurated by Viceroy Lord Irwin on February 12, 1931, to honor
soldiers lost in World War

3. lal kila

The Lal Qila (Red Fort) is a historic UNESCO World Heritage site in Delhi,
commissioned by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1638 and completed in 1648

4. Guru Duwra

Gurudwara Bangla Sahib is a prominent Sikh house of worship in New Delhi,
historically significant as the site where the eighth Guru, Guru Har
Krishan, stayed in 1664 and cured victims of a smallpox epidemic
using blessed water

5. Nalanda University Bihar

Nalanda University, founded in the 5th century CE, was the world’s
first residential international university.

6. Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh

The spiritual capital known for the Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat.

7. Manali, Himachal Pradesh

A popular destination for adventure activities like paragliding and trekking.

8. Darjeeling

Darjeeling, known as the "Queen of the Hills", is a renowned hill station
in West Bengal, India, situated at an elevation of approximately 6,700 feet.

9. Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh, located in the Himalayas, is a premier Indian tourist
destination known for hill stations like Shimla and Manali,
snow-capped peaks, adventure sports (paragliding, skiing), and spiritual sites.

10. Kedarnath

Kedarnath is a town and Nagar Panchayat in Rudraprayag district of
Uttarakhand, India, known primarily for the Kedarnath Temple.
