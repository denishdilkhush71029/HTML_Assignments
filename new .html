  • Albert Einstein:

Albert Einstein (1879–1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who revolutionized science with his theory of relativity (\(E=mc^{2}\)) and the photoelectric effect. Widely considered the most influential physicist of the 20th century, he won the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics. Fleeing Nazi Germany in 1933, he spent his later years at Princeton, becoming a U.S. citizen and advocating for peace.
